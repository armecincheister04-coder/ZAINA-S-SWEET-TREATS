Our History

Zaina’s Sweet Treats began in 2019 along Natalio Bacalso St., Barangay Granada, Boljoon, Cebu. What started as a small home baking hobby grew into a beloved cake shop known for its heartfelt flavors and creative designs.

From classic mango floats to custom-made cakes, every creation is made with care, premium ingredients, and a passion for sweet moments.

Today, Zaina’s Sweet Treats continues to spread happiness—one dessert at a time.

About the Owner

Zaina’s Sweet Treats is powered by the passion and hard work of its owner, Zaina Servilla. What began as her simple hobby in the kitchen slowly grew into something much bigger than she ever expected.

Starting with small orders from family, friends, and neighbors, Zaina spent countless hours learning new recipes, improving her skills, and perfecting each dessert she made. Her dedication, creativity, and love for making people happy pushed her to turn her hobby into a real business.

Today, her once small start has grown into a beloved cake shop. Through determination and faith, Zaina Servilla continues to create sweet treats that bring joy to birthdays, celebrations, and everyday moments—proving that big dreams truly start small.

Success Story Of The Business

Zaina’s Sweet Treats grew from a simple home baking hobby into a well-loved cake shop because of the dedication of its founder, Zaina Servilla. Through consistent quality, creativity, and genuine care for her customers, she transformed her passion into a thriving business.

From small neighborhood orders to larger celebrations and events, Zaina’s Sweet Treats has become known for its delicious flavors and beautifully crafted designs. Her success is a reflection of hard work, patience, and the belief that small beginnings can lead to big achievements.

Challenges Faced By The Founder/Owner

Before reaching success, Zaina Servilla faced many challenges. She started with limited equipment, a tight budget, and no guarantee that customers would trust her products. Every day was a test of strength—early mornings, late nights, and nonstop effort.

There were moments of slow sales, recipe failures, and difficult deliveries. But instead of giving up, Zaina used each struggle as motivation to improve her skills and strengthen her business. These challenges shaped her into the determined and passionate business owner she is today.

Message from the Owner to the Customers

Share how did the Philippine goverment help the business grow?

The Philippine government helped the business grow by offering support to small entrepreneurs through business registration assistance, livelihood programs, and training seminars provided by the DTI and local government units. These initiatives helped improve business skills, ensure proper compliance, and gave the confidence needed to expand and serve customers better.
